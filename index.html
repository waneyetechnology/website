Market Outlook

Forward-looking scenarios, key catalysts and indicators to watch

Short-term · 1–3 months

Markets face headwinds from oil-driven inflation, geopolitical tensions, and Fed policy uncertainty. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq are searching for direction ahead of Magnificent Seven earnings and Powell's final meeting. Energy and defense sectors should outperform, while consumer discretionary and high-multiple tech may underperform. Volatility is likely to remain elevated.

Long-term · 6–12 months

Prolonged energy supply disruption could reshape global trade flows, benefiting US energy producers and accelerating the energy transition. Fed leadership transition under Warsh may bring tighter monetary policy, favoring value and quality. AI infrastructure investment remains a multi-year theme, but with increasing scrutiny on capex efficiency. Housing and homebuilding offer long-term value due to structural supply shortages. Geopolitical realignment (UAE leaving OPEC, Israel defense sales) creates new investment opportunities.

Key Catalysts

  • Magnificent Seven earnings reports (April 29-30): Guidance on AI capex and revenue growth will set tech sector direction.
  • Fed policy decision and Powell's press conference (April 29-30): Rate hold expected, but forward guidance and Warsh nomination will be key.
  • Iran war developments: Any resolution or escalation in Hormuz blockade will significantly impact oil prices and inflation expectations.
  • UAE's exit from OPEC: Impact on oil supply dynamics and OPEC+ cohesion.
  • US gas prices: Continued rise above $4.23/gallon could further pressure consumer spending and Fed policy.
  • China's response to autonomous driving suspension: Regulatory clarity could impact global tech supply chains.
  • Blackstone's AI unit launch: Signals institutional capital flows into AI infrastructure.
  • Israel defense sale progress: Could boost defense sector and regional dynamics.
