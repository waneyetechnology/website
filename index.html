Market Outlook

Forward-looking scenarios, key catalysts and indicators to watch

Short-term · 1–3 months

Markets are in a corrective phase driven by AI profit-taking, trade policy uncertainty, and currency volatility. The US payrolls report will be a key catalyst; a strong print could lift yields and pressure equities, while a weak print may reinforce dovish Fed bets. Gold and yen are likely to remain supported. Oil may stabilize but faces downside risk from increased Hormuz flows. Tech selloff could deepen before a bottom forms.

Long-term · 6–12 months

AI infrastructure demand remains a multi-year growth driver, but near-term oversupply and valuation concerns warrant caution. Emerging markets, especially India and Brazil, offer structural growth opportunities. US trade policy under Trump (USMCA non-renewal, tariffs) will create persistent uncertainty. Inflation is moderating globally (Switzerland, US), supporting a gradual Fed pivot. Private credit and infrastructure investments are attractive for yield and diversification.

Key Catalysts

  • US June payrolls report (July 3)
  • Fed minutes and Warsh comments on rate path
  • Yen intervention by BOJ/MOF
  • Nike earnings and consumer spending data
  • USMCA renegotiation developments
  • AI earnings from major tech companies (Meta, Microsoft)
  • Oil supply disruptions from Hormuz or Iran
  • China-US tariff talks on agriculture
  • European heat wave impact on agriculture and energy
  • India budget and share sale plans

Monitor Closely

  • US 10-year Treasury yield and breakeven inflation rates
  • USD/JPY level and intervention triggers
  • Gold price and ETF flows
  • S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 technical support levels
  • VIX and volatility indices
  • Oil inventories and Strait of Hormuz transit data
  • AI-related ETF flows and short interest
  • Emerging market bond spreads
  • Swiss franc and yen as safe-haven proxies
  • Regulatory decisions on AI models (Anthropic Fable 5) and stablecoins
